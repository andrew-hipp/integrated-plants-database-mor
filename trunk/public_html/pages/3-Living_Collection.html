Living Collections of The Morton Arboretum

The Morton Arboretum living plant collections represent almost 90 years of plant acquisition and development. Plants in our collections have been acquired from both cultivated and wild sources from around the world, primarily in the northern temperate zone. The goal of collections at the Arboretum is to grow in its collections a broad range of plants native to the temperate regions of the world that can grow in the climate and soils of northern Illinois. The Arboretum maintains almost 4,300 different kinds of plants that are arranged in over 140 collections that have geographic, taxonomic, horticulture, landscape and special habitat themes.

Notable collections of elms, oaks, maples, crabapples, and extensive collections of Chinese, Appalachian, Ozarkian and Midwestern plants of wild collected origin are contained in the 688 hectares (1700 acres) of the Arboretum grounds. Also represented in our collections are accessions acquired from early botanists and plant explorers: Edgar Anderson (1897-1969), Emil Vasilievic Bretschneider (1833-1901), Benjamin Franklin Bush (1858-1937), Charles C. Deam (1865-1953), Joseph Hers (1884-1965), Elmer Drew Merrill (1876-1956), Frank N. Meyer (1875-1918), Ernest J. Palmer (1875-1962), William Purdom (1880-1921), Joseph Rock (1884-1962), Charles Sprague Sargent (1841-1927), Julian Steyermark (1909-1988) and Ernest H. Wilson (1876-1930).

The Arboretum is committed to advancing the knowledge of plants and conservation. To this end, it is an active member in the North America - China Plant Collections Consortium (NACPEC), the Plant Collecting Collaborative (PCC), and the North American Plant Collections Consortium (NAPCC). As part of these and other organizations, the Arboretum has participated in seed collecting expeditions to China, the Republic of Georgia, the Appalachians, and the Ozarks. Arboretum collecting trips have also gone to the Pacific Northwest, Rocky Mountains, Black Hills, Great Plains and Midwest. Many plants found in our collections are the results of these collecting trips, and the information about them accessed through this site.

Living Collections additional information and contacts

Just like the plants in our care, the Living Collections of The Morton Arboretum are living, growing and ever-changing. Use this site as an introduction to our collections and grounds, but, please visit us if you are able. The information and images presented here cannot compare to the 690 hectares (1700 acres) of plant collections from around the world, native woodlands and extensive Schulenberg Prairie and Savanna restoration that are open to our visitors.

If you need further information from our extensive plant records system you may contact Ed Hedborn, Manager of Plant Records. If you need information about living plants in the Collections, plant problems or other horticulturally related questions you may contact our Plant Clinic.
